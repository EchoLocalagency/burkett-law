My Approach

Family law is different from every other legal practice because the decisions shape the rest of your life and the lives of the people you love most. I built my practice around a simple idea: clients deserve steady representation from someone who returns their calls, explains what is happening, and treats the case with the seriousness it warrants.

Because I practice on my own, the attorney you meet at the first consultation is the attorney who handles your case from the first filing through the final order. Nothing gets handed off. Nothing gets lost in translation between a partner and an associate. You get one point of contact, and I make sure that point of contact is reliable.

I try to explain family law in plain language. California family court has its own vocabulary — petitioner, respondent, ex parte, Request for Order — and part of my job is translating that language into what it actually means for you and your family. I would rather over-explain than leave a client guessing.

What I Handle

My practice covers the full range of California family law matters:

  • Divorce — including uncontested, contested, and higher-asset dissolutions.
  • Child custody — legal and physical custody, parenting plans, and modifications.
  • Child support — California guideline calculations, modification, and enforcement.
  • Spousal support — temporary and long-term orders under California law.
  • Mediation — as counsel or as neutral mediator, depending on the matter.
  • Domestic violence — restraining orders and the family-law issues that follow.
  • Guardianship — probate and juvenile guardianship of minors.
  • Family court navigation — general assistance with the family court process itself.

If a matter falls outside family law, I will tell you at the first consultation and, when possible, point you to a colleague who handles it.
